May 1st, 2014

When I think of the times in my life when I have been the happiest, proudest or most satisfied, the following values come to mind: accomplishment, adventure, close relationships, creativity, excellence, fairness, freedom, self-respect, knowledge, power and wisdom.

The value that I identify with most

Of these values, one reigns supreme. Most of my proudest moments are centered around close relationships. Whether I accomplished something great or I went on a wild adventure, none of those things would be the same without close relationships.

I remember getting good grades as a kid. For me, the actual act of getting a good grade was very rewarding. However, sharing that experience with my family was much more rewarding. The same rule applied to my competitve swimming career. Winning a race was nice, but if you didn't have people to celebrate with, then it would not have been quite as enjoyable.

Rating the values:

I will now be rating the values that I have chosen based on whether or not I agree that I try to live up to these values. 1 being that I don't try to live up to the value and 5 that I fully try to live up to the value.

  • Accomplishment-5
  • Adventure-5
  • Fairness-5
  • Excellence-5
  • Close relationships-5
  • Creativity-5
  • Freedom-5
  • Self-Respect-5
  • Knowledge-5
  • Power-5
  • Wisdom-5

It might seem a little bit boring that I gave myself a perfect 5 for each of these values. When I truly look within myself, I feel I 100% agree that I try to embody each of these values on a daily basis. Am I perfect at doing so, of course not. I still try to strive to meet these values and various other values.
