Values Revisited

If you have stuck with me throughout Phase 0, you'll remember that I wrote about the following values:accomplishment, adventure, close relationships, creativity, excellence, fairness, freedom, self-respect, knowledge, power and wisdom. For whatever reason, these values seemed to jump out me from the given list. A few weeks have passed and I am now reviewing this list and it still holds true. Seeing this values again helps me understand how I truly view myself. In terms of programming, I think it will help me if I ever notice that I am in stereotype threat

How I will use these values for good:

For the most part, I am a fairly positive person. I also work hard for everything I have in life and I have been relatively successful at everything that I strive for. Regardless, there are still moments when a negative thought or stereotype threat may creep into my mind.

Moving forward I will be constantly reminding myself of what I am capable of when I feel negative thoughts coming on. If I find myself sensing stereotype threat, I will choose to not let those thoughts linger. Instead I can reaffirm what I stand for and how I will acheive my goals of becoming a programmer.

Other Jedi Mind Tricks

So what other exercises can I use if I am feeling down at DBC? Well, I hope this isn't an issue, but if I feel down I will use a combination of methods to resolve the issue. I tend to run/lift when I am extremely stressed. Nothing relieves stress like a good workout. In addition to that, I have been getting into meditation more and more over the last few months. Meditation seems to help a great deal with my overall wellness.

Aside from that, all I can do is eat well and tell myself that everything will be ok. Afterall, it will be.
